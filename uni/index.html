Online gambling complaints on the ri$e

British online gambling companies are facing a rising tide of customer dissatisfaction. Complaints logged by online gambling operators increased by more than ten times between 2014 and 2015 and have continued to grow, according to a decade’s worth of company regulatory return data acquired via FOI from the UK Gambling Commission.

Complaints in the “remote casino, betting and bingo” sector, or online gambling, earned 47 percent of complaints among all sectors in the last decade. But the last three years took 41 percent of complaints in the same period.

In 2017, more than 100,000 customers complained about their online gambling experience, becoming the highest year on record and earning 37 percent of remote gambling complaints in the last decade, with the last three years accounting for 87 percent.

March 2017 complaints process in the gambling industry report, chief executive Sarah Harrison: “In the last year we received nearly 77,000 contacts from members of the public. This is an increase of over 300 per cent on the last two years. It tells us that existing complaints arrangements in the sector are not meeting consumer needs.”

The complaints come at a time when the UK Gambling Commission has issued more than £18 million in fines against gambling companies since 2016. The fines are centered around deceptive advertising and social responsibility, which allows gamblers to voluntarily opt-out, or “self-exclude,” from gambling offers.
